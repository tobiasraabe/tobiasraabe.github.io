Research

Malleability of Locus of Control

This research project is concerned with a person's Locus of Control which is the degree to which people believe that they have control over the outcome of events in their lives. We use the GSOEP (German Socio-Economic Panel) from 2005-2015 and estimate in a difference-and-difference design the changes in Locus of Control due to potentially exogenous and negative events like death of a close relative. The reasoning is that sudden, negative events raise the salience of lack of power which might push the individual towards a more external Locus of Control. External in contrast to internal Locus of Control means that the individual believes outcomes cannot be controlled by the individual whereas internal means the opposite.
